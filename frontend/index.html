🩸 Blood Pressure (BP)

Blood pressure is the force of circulating blood on the walls of arteries. It is measured in mmHg and has two key values:

  • Systolic Pressure (upper value): The pressure when the heart contracts and pumps blood into the arteries. It reflects how strongly the heart is working.
  • Diastolic Pressure (lower value): The pressure when the heart relaxes between beats. It shows the baseline resistance in the arteries.

👉 Why Important?
Normal BP is around 120/80 mmHg. High BP (Hypertension) can damage arteries, increasing risk of heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease. Low BP (Hypotension) can cause dizziness, fainting, and inadequate blood supply to organs.

❤️ Heart Rate (HR)

Heart rate is the number of times the heart beats per minute (bpm). It reflects how hard the heart is working to pump blood through the body.

  • Normal Resting HR: 60–100 bpm for adults.
  • Athletes: Often have a lower HR (<60 bpm) because of stronger heart function.

👉 Why Important?
Heart rate is a direct indicator of cardiovascular health. Continuous abnormal HR may signal underlying issues like arrhythmia, thyroid imbalance, or heart disease.

😟 Stress Level

Stress is the body’s physical and mental response to demands or challenges. It triggers the release of hormones like adrenaline and cortisol, which affect heart and blood pressure.

Physiological Signs of Stress include increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, and reduced Heart Rate Variability (HRV).

  • Short-Term Stress: Can improve alertness and energy (fight-or-flight response).
  • Chronic Stress: Over time, it contributes to hypertension, heart disease, diabetes, obesity, and mental health issues.

👉 Why Important?
Monitoring stress helps prevent long-term health risks and improves overall well-being. Techniques like relaxation, exercise, meditation, and sleep help manage stress effectively.
